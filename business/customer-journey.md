# Customer Journey Map
## Overview
The customer journey map for stock-sync outlines the phases a potential customer goes through from initial awareness to expansion of the product. The map highlights trigger events, friction points, user emotions, opportunities to delight, and key metrics for each phase.

## Aware
* **Trigger Event**: High inventory holding costs, stockouts, or overstocking issues in their current DTC assortment
* **Friction Points**: Lack of visibility into inventory levels, inefficient manual forecasting methods
* **User Emotions**: Frustration, anxiety about potential losses due to inventory mismanagement
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Educational content (blog posts, webinars) on the benefits of AI-powered inventory planning, highlighting the potential for cost savings and improved efficiency
* **Metric**: Website traffic, social media engagement, lead generation (e.g., 500 new leads per quarter)

## Consider
* **Trigger Event**: Research into inventory management solutions, potentially discovering stock-sync through online search, recommendations, or industry events
* **Friction Points**: Comparing features and pricing among different solutions, concern about integration with existing systems
* **User Emotions**: Curiosity, skepticism about the effectiveness of AI in inventory planning
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Free trials or demos, case studies of similar businesses that have successfully implemented stock-sync, clear and transparent pricing information
* **Metric**: Trial sign-ups (e.g., 100 trials per month), sales-qualified leads (e.g., 20 SQLs per quarter)

## Try
* **Trigger Event**: Signing up for a free trial or demo of stock-sync
* **Friction Points**: Initial setup and integration challenges, learning curve for using the AI-powered forecasting tool
* **User Emotions**: Excitement about potential benefits, apprehension about the learning process
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Prompt and helpful customer support, intuitive user interface, clear documentation and tutorials
* **Metric**: Trial-to-paid conversion rate (e.g., 30% conversion rate), average time to first successful forecast (e.g., 3 days)

## Adopt
* **Trigger Event**: Decision to purchase stock-sync after a successful trial or demo
* **Friction Points**: Potential issues with payment processing, need for ongoing support and training
* **User Emotions**: Satisfaction with the decision, anticipation of improved inventory management
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Seamless onboarding process, regular software updates with new features, responsive customer support
* **Metric**: Customer acquisition cost (CAC) payback period (e.g., 6 months), customer health score (e.g., 85% of customers are "healthy")

## Expand
* **Trigger Event**: Successful implementation and use of stock-sync, potential for upselling or cross-selling other Axentx products
* **Friction Points**: Potential complacency with current inventory management processes, lack of awareness about additional features or products
* **User Emotions**: Loyalty, trust in the brand and its products
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Regular account management check-ins, tailored recommendations for additional products or features, loyalty programs
* **Metric**: Customer lifetime value (CLV) to CAC ratio (e.g., 3:1), net promoter score (NPS) (e.g., 40)

By understanding and addressing the needs and emotions of customers at each phase of the journey, stock-sync can increase user satisfaction, reduce churn, and ultimately drive business growth.
